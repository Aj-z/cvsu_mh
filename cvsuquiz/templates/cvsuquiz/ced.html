Bachelor of Secondary Education

Prepares future educators to teach at the secondary level by specializing in academic disciplines like Mathematics, English, Filipino, Science, or Social Studies. The program emphasizes mastery of subject matter, innovative teaching techniques, and effective classroom management strategies.

Students develop skills in curriculum design, educational research, and technology integration to engage adolescent learners. The program fosters critical thinking, adaptability, and strong communication skills to address the challenges of secondary education.

Graduates can work as high school teachers, academic coordinators, educational consultants, or instructional material developers. Opportunities in online teaching, tutoring, or roles in education-focused non-governmental organizations are also available.

Bachelor of Elementary Education

Focuses on preparing future educators to teach at the elementary level by developing a deep understanding of pedagogy, curriculum design, and child psychology. Students gain knowledge in foundational subjects like mathematics, science, language, and social studies, alongside effective teaching strategies and classroom management.

The program fosters creativity, critical thinking, and empathy, emphasizing the role of educators in shaping young minds. Graduates develop skills in lesson planning, assessment, and educational technology, equipping them to address diverse learning needs and promote inclusive education.

Career opportunities include roles as elementary school teachers, curriculum developers, instructional designers, or school administrators. Graduates can also explore opportunities in educational consulting, tutoring, and creating online learning materials.

Bachelor of Science in Hotel and Restaurant Management (BSHRM)

Focuses on developing expertise in hospitality operations, including hotel management, culinary arts, and customer service. The program combines theoretical knowledge with hands-on training in areas like food and beverage management, housekeeping, and front office operations.

Students gain skills in business management, event planning, and marketing, with a strong emphasis on professionalism, teamwork, and cultural awareness. The program prepares graduates to meet global hospitality standards and adapt to emerging industry trends.

Career paths include roles as hotel managers, chefs, restaurant owners, event coordinators, or travel and tourism consultants. Graduates can also explore opportunities in luxury resorts, cruise ships, and other international hospitality establishments.

Bachelor of Science in Tourism Management

Equips students with the knowledge and skills needed for success in the dynamic tourism and hospitality industry. The program covers tourism planning, destination management, customer service, and global trends in travel. Students learn through experiential activities, internships, and field trips that enhance their practical understanding.

The curriculum emphasizes sustainability, cultural sensitivity, and effective communication, preparing graduates to promote responsible tourism. Skills in event planning, marketing, and business management are developed to meet industry demands.

Graduates can pursue careers as travel consultants, tourism officers, event planners, or hospitality managers. Opportunities also include roles in airlines, cruise lines, or entrepreneurial ventures in travel-related businesses.
