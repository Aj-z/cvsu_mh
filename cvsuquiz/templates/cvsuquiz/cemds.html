Bachelor of Science in Office Administration

Prepares students for administrative and clerical roles by developing expertise in office systems, business communication, and organizational management. The program emphasizes proficiency in office technologies, documentation, and professional etiquette.

Students gain skills in record-keeping, event coordination, and customer relations, with a strong focus on time management and workplace efficiency. Practical training, internships, and case studies prepare graduates to thrive in modern office environments.

Career opportunities include administrative assistant, office manager, executive secretary, or virtual assistant roles. Graduates can also explore opportunities in customer support, data management, or office systems consulting.

Bachelor of Science in Accountancy

Offers comprehensive training in financial accounting, auditing, and taxation to prepare students for professional accounting careers. The program emphasizes analytical thinking, problem-solving, and adherence to ethical standards in financial reporting.

Students gain expertise in preparing and interpreting financial statements, conducting audits, and managing budgets. The curriculum includes practical applications, case studies, and training in accounting software.

Graduates can pursue careers as certified public accountants (CPAs), auditors, financial analysts, or tax consultants. Opportunities also exist in corporate finance, banking, and public sector accounting.

  • CMO: Policies, Standard And Guidelines for Accountancy

Bachelor of Business Administration

Provides a broad foundation in business principles, including management, marketing, finance, and entrepreneurship. The program emphasizes leadership, strategic thinking, and decision-making skills to address organizational challenges.

Students develop expertise in business planning, resource management, and market analysis, supported by real-world applications and internships. The curriculum fosters adaptability and innovation in a competitive global market.

Career paths include business manager, marketing specialist, financial advisor, or entrepreneur. Graduates can also explore roles in operations management or business consulting.

Bachelor of Science in Economics

Explores economic theory, policy, and data analysis to understand and solve complex financial and social issues. The program combines quantitative skills with critical thinking to analyze market trends and inform decision-making.

Students learn about microeconomics, macroeconomics, econometrics, and international trade, gaining tools for interpreting data and forecasting economic outcomes. The curriculum includes case studies and research projects for practical application.

Graduates can pursue careers as economists, financial analysts, policy advisors, or market researchers. Opportunities also exist in government, international organizations, and academic research.

Bachelor of Science in International Studies

Prepares students to understand global affairs, diplomacy, and cultural diversity, emphasizing international relations and global issues. The program explores topics like international politics, economics, and human rights, fostering cross-cultural awareness.

Students gain skills in research, negotiation, and policy analysis, supported by language training and internships in international organizations. The curriculum prepares graduates to engage effectively in global contexts.

Career opportunities include roles as diplomats, international consultants, foreign service officers, or policy analysts. Graduates can also pursue careers in non-governmental organizations or multinational corporations.
