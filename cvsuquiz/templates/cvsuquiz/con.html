Bachelor of Science in Medical Technology

This program delves into the science, technology, and practices of agriculture, equipping students with knowledge in crop and livestock production, soil management, agricultural economics, and sustainable farming practices. Students gain hands-on experience through laboratory and fieldwork, exploring innovative agricultural techniques and addressing global food security challenges.

The program fosters skills in critical thinking, problem-solving, and research, preparing graduates to develop solutions for sustainable agriculture. With expertise in farming systems, agribusiness, and resource management, graduates are well-equipped to navigate the complexities of modern agriculture.

Career opportunities include roles in farm management, agricultural research, agribusiness, government agencies, and consultancy. Graduates may also pursue entrepreneurial ventures or global opportunities in agriculture-related industries.

Bachelor of Science in Midwifery

This program focuses on the application of modern technology to agricultural processes, combining technical training with a strong foundation in agricultural science. Students learn about agricultural engineering, post-harvest technology, animal production, and agribusiness operations, preparing them to implement practical solutions to agricultural challenges.

The curriculum emphasizes innovation, hands-on technical skills, and adaptability, empowering students to address the technological demands of the agriculture sector. Graduates emerge with the expertise to improve productivity and efficiency in farming and agribusiness systems.

Career paths include roles in agricultural engineering, farm management, agribusiness, technical consultancy, and agricultural extension. Graduates can also explore entrepreneurial opportunities in mechanized farming and agricultural innovation.

Bachelor of Science in Nursing

This program investigates the complex relationships between natural systems, human activities, and environmental sustainability. Students study ecology, environmental policy, waste management, climate change, and conservation biology, gaining a multidisciplinary perspective on environmental challenges.

Through fieldwork, research, and practical projects, students develop analytical, research, and advocacy skills. The program prepares graduates to address critical environmental issues, promote sustainability, and implement policies to protect ecosystems.

Graduates can pursue careers in environmental consultancy, conservation, research, education, or government agencies. Opportunities also exist in sustainable development projects, renewable energy, and global environmental initiatives.
